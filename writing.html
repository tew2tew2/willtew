Metis Machines: Community Memory and the Politics of Computing

M.A. thesis that tracks the history of the Community Memory network in Berkeley, C.A. Significant as one of the first computer-based BBS systems. Its history troubles the standard history of computing in the designer's attempts to question the political and militarized power latent within computing technology. Read more, download here

“Infrastrukturproteste” and the German Autonomous Movement: The Struggle Against “Startbahn-West” and Logistics in the Late 20th Century

B.A. thesis which pairs a narrative overview of the "Startbahn-West" movement against the expansion of Germany's Frankfurt Airport with a historical account of postwar capitalism which emphasizes the role of logistical technology. By pairing these two analyses I hoped to tease out the ways in which the German protestors implicitly understood the changing nature of capitalist development. Read more, download here
